Language: Dutch
Abstract:

In this letter Edward Upward discusses alternative titles for his new book, The Borderline, with his publisher Mr Leonard Woolf.



Streatham S.W.16 , Nov 17th 1937


Dear Mr Woolf,


I am sorry for my delay
in answering your last letter. I have been
trying to think of another title for The Borderline
and I have not yet discovered anything really
satisfactory. The best I can suggest is 'Journey
to the Border', or if that is too near to the original
title perhaps 'Descent from the Race course' would
do-though I don't very much like it.

I have not had a free evening for some time
and have not yet made the necessary corrections
in the typescript, but I will do so to-morrow and
will post the typescript on Friday.


Yours sincerely,

Edward Upward

Edward Upward 39, Woodleigh Gardens
